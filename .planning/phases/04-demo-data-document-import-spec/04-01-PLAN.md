<!-- DOC-02 mandatory fields per REQUIREMENTS.md (also constrained by
     entity.document.schema.json). Every metadata.yaml AND every
     Document entity YAML must populate these. -->
DOC-02 mandatory:
  title (string)
  doc_type (enum: regulation | standard | paper | report | manual | accident_report | internal_note)
  language (enum: zh | en | mixed)
  source_url (URI)
  publication_date (ISO date YYYY-MM-DD)
  effective_date (ISO date YYYY-MM-DD)
  confidentiality (enum: public | internal | restricted | itar_ear)
  domain_tags (array of kebab-case strings)
  version (string)            # for the Document entity field is `document_version`
  file_hash (sha256:<hex>)
  processed_by (string — tool used; e.g. "manual-pandoc-3.x")

URI scheme:
  External: aviationkb://<type>/<slug>@<version>
  Person prefix: pers, e.g. aviationkb://person/jane-reviewer@1
  Organization prefix: org, e.g. aviationkb://organization/faa@1
  Document prefix: doc, e.g. aviationkb://document/far-25-1309@1

Provenance mandatory (PROV-01):
  method (human | ai_extracted | hybrid_reviewed)
  actor (Person or Organization URI)
  actor_role (free string ≥1 char)
  created_at (RFC 3339 timestamp)

Confidence mandatory (PROV-02):
  score (0.0–1.0)
  rationale (≥20 chars)

Source mandatory (PROV-03) — even on Person/Organization/Document records:
  document_id (Document URI)
  locator (page | section | paragraph — ≥1)
  retrieval (url + retrieved_at)

NOTE: a Document entity's `source.document_id` MAY point at itself —
that is the convention the existing test fixture
tests/fixtures/valid/entities/document_far-25-1309.yaml uses. Person and
Organization records cite the Document they were sourced from (e.g. the
FAA org cites the FAR Part 25 document its data was transcribed from).
</interfaces>

# FAR §25.1309 — Equipment, systems, and installations (stand-in)

> Stand-in for the original eCFR / Federal Register PDF. Replace with the
> actual PDF via Git LFS when ingestion goes live. Source URL:
> https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-25/subpart-F/section-25.1309

The equipment, systems, and installations whose functioning is required by
this subchapter must be designed to ensure that they perform their
intended functions under any foreseeable operating condition.

The airplane systems and associated components, considered separately and
in relation to other systems, must be designed so that the occurrence of
any failure condition which would prevent the continued safe flight and
landing of the airplane is extremely improbable, and the occurrence of any
other failure conditions which would reduce the capability of the airplane
or the ability of the crew to cope with adverse operating conditions is
improbable.

Information concerning unsafe system operating conditions must be provided
to the crew to enable them to take appropriate corrective action. A means
must be provided to inform the crew of unsafe system operating conditions,
and to enable them to take appropriate corrective action.
