## (a) General

The equipment, systems, and installations whose functioning is required by
14 CFR Part 25 must be designed to ensure that they perform their intended
functions under any foreseeable operating condition. This is the umbrella
clause that anchors the entire Part 25 system-safety methodology.

## (b) Failure-condition-probability matrix

The airplane systems and associated components, considered separately and in
relation to other systems, must be designed such that:

- **Catastrophic** failure conditions (preventing continued safe flight and
  landing) are **extremely improbable** — ≤1×10⁻⁹ per flight hour.
- **Hazardous** failure conditions are **extremely remote** —
  ≤1×10⁻⁷ per flight hour.
- **Major** failure conditions are **remote** — ≤1×10⁻⁵ per flight hour.
- **Minor** failure conditions are **probable** — ≤1×10⁻³ per flight hour.

These tiers are referenced by AC 25.1309-1A and SAE ARP4761 and form the
quantitative bridge between Functional Hazard Assessment (FHA) and Fault
Tree Analysis (FTA).

## (c) Crew alerting

Information concerning unsafe system operating conditions must be provided
to the crew to enable them to take appropriate corrective action. The means
provided must:

- Inform the crew of unsafe system operating conditions,
- Enable the crew to take appropriate corrective action,
- Conform to the flight-crew alerting requirements of §25.1322.
