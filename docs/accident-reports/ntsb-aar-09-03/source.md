# NTSB AAR-09/03 — Loss of Control on Approach, Colgan Air Flight 3407 (stand-in)

> Excerpt of: NTSB/AAR-10/01 (the AAR-09/03 docket), Executive Summary +
> Probable Cause section.

On February 12, 2009, about 22:17 Eastern Standard Time, Colgan Air
flight 3407, a Bombardier DHC-8-400 (Q400) operating as a Continental
Connection scheduled passenger flight from Newark, New Jersey, to
Buffalo, New York, crashed into a residence in Clarence Center, New
York, during an instrument approach to runway 23 at Buffalo-Niagara
International Airport. The 2 pilots, 2 flight attendants, and 45
passengers aboard the airplane were fatally injured, along with 1
person on the ground. The airplane was destroyed by impact forces and
post-crash fire. Night visual meteorological conditions with snow and
mist prevailed at the time of the accident.

The flight was operated under the provisions of 14 CFR Part 121. Icing
conditions had been forecast for the route and were encountered during
the descent. The captain's responses to the activation of the stick
shaker — pulling aft on the control column rather than executing the
proper stall-recovery procedure (push the column forward to reduce
angle of attack) — were inconsistent with his training and with the
manufacturer's procedures. The stick pusher activated and the captain
overrode it.

The National Transportation Safety Board determined that the probable
cause of this accident was the captain's inappropriate response to the
activation of the stick shaker, which led to an aerodynamic stall from
which the airplane did not recover. Contributing factors included the
flight crew's failure to monitor airspeed in relation to the rising
position of the low-speed cue, failure to adhere to sterile cockpit
procedures, the captain's failure to effectively manage the flight,
and Colgan Air's inadequate procedures for airspeed selection and
management during approaches in icing conditions.
