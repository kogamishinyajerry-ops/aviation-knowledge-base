## Executive Summary

On February 12, 2009, Colgan Air flight 3407 (DHC-8-400, registration
N200WQ) crashed on approach to Buffalo-Niagara International Airport,
killing all 49 people on board plus 1 person on the ground. The flight
was operating as Continental Connection from Newark to Buffalo under
14 CFR Part 121. Night IMC with snow and mist; icing forecast and
encountered during descent.

## Sequence of events

- ~22:14 EST — Flaps 10 selected; airspeed bleeding off.
- ~22:16 EST — Stick shaker activates as the reference airspeed
  (V_REF) approached the low-speed cue.
- The captain pulled aft on the control column (incorrect input).
- The stick pusher activated; the captain overrode it.
- Aerodynamic stall and loss of control followed.
- The airplane impacted a residence in Clarence Center at ~22:17 EST.

## Findings (selected)

- The flight crew did not monitor airspeed in relation to the
  rising low-speed cue.
- The captain's response to the stick shaker was inconsistent with his
  training and with the Bombardier Q400 stall-recovery procedure.
- Colgan Air's procedures for airspeed selection and management during
  approaches in icing conditions were inadequate.
- The captain and first officer did not adhere to sterile-cockpit
  procedures during the approach.

## Probable Cause

The captain's inappropriate response to the activation of the stick
shaker, which led to an aerodynamic stall from which the airplane did
not recover.

### Contributing factors

- Flight crew's failure to monitor airspeed.
- Failure to adhere to sterile-cockpit procedures.
- Captain's failure to effectively manage the flight.
- Colgan Air's inadequate procedures for icing-condition approaches.

## Selected safety recommendations

- Recommendations to FAA on stall-recovery training for Part 121
  carriers (basis for FAA's later Stall Prevention and Recovery rule).
- Recommendations on airspeed-monitoring procedures during icing.
- Recommendations on pilot fatigue management (basis for the 2011
  Part 117 flight/duty/rest rule).
