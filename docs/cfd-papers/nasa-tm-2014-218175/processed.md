## Abstract

The Spalart-Allmaras (SA) one-equation turbulence model is re-implemented
and verified in the NASA OVERFLOW structured-grid RANS solver against
canonical cases from the NASA Langley Turbulence Modeling Resource (TMR).
Verification covers the 2D flat plate, bump-in-channel, and NACA 0012
airfoil. Validation extends to the 3D ONERA-M6 transonic wing.

## 1. Introduction

The SA model (Spalart & Allmaras, 1992) remains the de-facto industry
default for steady-state external-aerodynamics RANS simulations because
its single-equation form is computationally cheap and numerically robust.
Periodic re-verification against TMR is necessary because solver
infrastructure (flux schemes, limiters, time-integration) evolves
independently from the turbulence-model formulation.

## 2. Verification matrix

The verification suite consists of three canonical 2D cases:

- **2D zero-pressure-gradient flat plate** — five grids 35×25 → 545×385.
- **2D bump-in-channel** — verifies pressure-gradient sensitivity.
- **2D NACA 0012, Re=6e6, M=0.15, α=0°/10°/15°** — verifies separated-flow
  prediction; finest grid 1.7M cells at wall y⁺ ≤ 1.

## 3. Verification results

Computed C_f, C_p, and integrated C_l / C_d agree with the TMR reference
CFL3D and FUN3D solutions to within ±0.5% on the finest grid in each case.
Grid-convergence indices (GCI) per Roache (1994) are tabulated for each
integrated quantity.

## 4. Validation — ONERA-M6 wing

The 3D ONERA-M6 wing at M=0.84, α=3.06°, Re=11.7×10⁶ is used for
validation. C_p comparisons at seven spanwise stations (η = 0.20, 0.44,
0.65, 0.80, 0.90, 0.95, 0.99) agree with the Schmitt & Charpin (AGARD
AR-138) experimental data to within experimental uncertainty.

## 5. Conclusion

The OVERFLOW implementation of the SA model meets TMR verification
criteria and reproduces ONERA-M6 validation data within experimental
scatter. The SA-neg modification (Allmaras et al., 2012) is recommended
as the default to improve robustness on coarse grids.
